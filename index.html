Overview

The Unified Bharat Framework is an independent research project documenting India's major policy, governance, legal, cultural, and infrastructure transformations from 2014 to 2025. It brings together timelines, policy clusters, reform categories, and data-backed insights across multiple sectors.

The Five Core Pillars

  • Article 370 Abrogation (2019)
  • Triple Talaq Criminalization (2019)
  • UCC Uttarakhand (2024)
  • Anti-polygamy legislation
  • Marriage law reforms
  • Anti-conversion laws (9+ states)
  • Population policy discussions
  • Family structure standardization
  • Registration reforms
  • GST Council - One Nation One Tax
  • Ayushman Bharat - 55 crore beneficiaries
  • One Nation One Ration Card
  • JAM Trinity (Jan Dhan-Aadhaar-Mobile)
  • NEP 2020 - Unified education
  • Ram Mandir Consecration (2024)
  • Kashi Vishwanath Corridor (2021)
  • Statue of Unity (2018)

  • New Parliament Building (2023)
  • Bharatmala - 83,677 km highways
  • Bullet Train (under construction)
  • Dedicated Freight Corridors
  • Smart Cities Mission (100 cities)
  • Ayodhya Development - ₹85,000 cr

Scope of This Research

The project includes more than 150 documented initiatives across sectors such as law, economy, health, welfare, culture, defense, and national identity. Publicly available data from official government sources is used to build timelines and comparative indicators.

About the Author
